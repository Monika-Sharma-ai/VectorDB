KD-Tree — Axis-Aligned Space Partitioning

Recursively split points along one axis at a time, like cutting a cake with straight knives.

Intuition Pick an axis → split at the median → recurse. Build a binary tree where each node owns a half-space.

Step 1Pick axis (alternate x, y, x, ...)
Step 2Split at median on that axis
Step 3Recurse on left & right halves
Step 4Leaves hold a few points

Build: O(N log N) · Best for: low-dim data (≤ ~20 dims).

Ball Tree — Nested Hyperspheres

Wrap groups of points in nested balls; prune any ball that's too far from Q.

Intuition Each node owns a sphere covering its points. Skip a sphere entirely when dist(Q, center) − radius > best_so_far.

  • Not axis-aligned → handles diagonal & rotated data better than KD-Tree
  • Works with any distance metric (cosine, Mahalanobis, Haversine)
  • Build O(N log N), query ~O(log N) in moderate dims

Pruning rule is the whole game — a tight ball prunes more aggressively.

KD-Tree vs Ball Tree — When Trees Break Down

Both are exact and elegant — but neither survives high-dimensional embeddings.

KD-TreeBall Tree

Use trees for: 2D/3D points, geo-spatial, small feature vectors, classical ML.

Don't use for: 384-d / 768-d / 1536-d transformer embeddings → use HNSW or IVF+PQ.
